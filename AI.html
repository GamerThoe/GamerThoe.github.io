AI In Schools

To BAN or not to BAN

Now that AI is here, what do we do as educators? Should we block it out, ban it from our classrooms, and hope to teach our students to Just Say No? Though currently RUSD has NOT elected to BAN the technology, they have removed the ability for students to log in using their google suite accounts. There are growing sentiments among my colleagues to remove the technology (like ChatGPT) from the classroom, something that I disagree with completely. If you are interested how I am adapting to this technology in my classroom, here are some key points to consider:


  • Students who have access to ChatGPT will use it at home
  • Students who DO NOT have access to ChatGPT at home will be at a disadvantage -- this is not equitable
  • Assignments that can be done eaily with ChatGPT are most likely not rigorous assignments
  • Students know the difference between right and wrong

Benefits of AI

Aside from the points above, what about all the benefits AI has to offer in the classroom?

  • Individual Tutor for every student
  • A tool to help students learn to articulate what they are looking for or need help with
  • The chance to teach students how to use AI tools responsibly
  • The ability for AI to remember and adapt to the needs and previous student inquiries
  • Arming students with the newest tools in tech available

Conclusion

I'm of the opinion that this technology is here to stay. Rather than pretend we can actually stop students from using it, for those people who wish that was possible, I believe it is imperative to teach students how to use it responsibly. Nobody knows for sure what will come of AI or if it will take over our world (in education or in general) but there are benefits to using this technology now and adapting to a world with AI is something we can do. As educators we have a duty to prepare our students for the future and how to use things like ChatGPT ethically. The world has changed, and it is important that we change too.
